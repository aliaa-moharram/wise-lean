01

Pain points & opportunities

Wise runs on local rails in its other markets - direct debit, local schemes, and card networks. Bringing the same product experience to the UAE means moving funding, recurring pulls, and payouts onto local account-to-account infrastructure. These are the remittance pain points Lean addresses, split by business and technical impact.

Business impact

Pain point / opportunityHow Lean solves itAdvantage
Card fees erode thin margins on top-ups and collections. Lean Pay by Bank for bank-to-bank top-ups and collections as a lower-cost alternative to cards. Lower take-rates, improved unit economics, and more pricing flexibility per corridor.
Volatile rates, spreads, and correspondent fees vary by corridor, squeezing competitiveness. Reduce card dependence, verify beneficiaries to avoid costly bounce-backs, and improve settlement predictability. Higher, more stable margins per corridor through lower acceptance costs and fewer payout failures.
Wrong or mismatched beneficiary details cause rejections, bounce-backs, and manual handling. Account Verification confirms recipient account ownership before funds move. Fewer rejections and bounce-backs, faster delivery, lower ops cost, higher trust.

Technical & operational

Pain point / opportunityHow Lean solves itAdvantage
Multiple payment methods and payout rails increase integration and ops complexity. A single integration for Pay by Bank, Payouts, and Account Verification, with normalized references and clear statuses across banks. Faster time-to-market and leaner operations, with less engineering effort and support load.
Bank variability and inconsistent references make for a non-standard experience across banks and corridors. Consistent payment-intent references and verification before payouts, handling different bank formats. More predictable ops and faster exception handling across markets.
Heavy AML / KYC expectations, especially source-of-funds and beneficiary ownership checks, add operational burden. Verification and identity signals streamline ownership checks in onboarding and payout flows. Reduced manual document collection, better compliance posture, smoother UX.

Top up from your bank

Lean powers it
SIPAccount-on-FileSIP for one-off top-ups; an AoF consent for repeat top-ups (authorise once, debit on demand).
Journey 2 Recurring funding

Money on autopilot

Wise feature
Recurring, scheduled top-ups, for example automating savings into a Jar on a set cadence.
The need
Keep the Wise balance funded on schedule, without asking the customer each time.
Lean powers it
Account-on-FileOne consent, many scheduled debits. Ahead of each scheduled run, Wise calls the AoF mandate to top up the balance. What happens next is handled inside Wise: an internal move (a savings Jar) stays put, while an outbound send goes out as a payout (Journey 3).

03

Lean product coverage

What each Lean product does for Wise in the UAE.

Core

Single Instant Payment

One-off account-to-account pay-in (SIP) that funds the Wise balance directly from the customer's bank.

Core

Account-on-File

Consent once, debit many. The engine behind repeat top-ups and every scheduled or recurring transfer.

Core

Payouts

Outbound transfers to UAE bank accounts and billers, initiated server-side.

Core

Account Verification

Verifies a beneficiary's account and name before a payout leaves. Extends internationally across 25+ countries, covering Wise's outbound corridors.

Extension

Payment Links

Hosted Pay by Bank via link or QR to power Request money / Scan-to-pay.
